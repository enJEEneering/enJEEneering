National Science Examination in Physics(NSEP)

By Karthikeya Reddy Konda, AIR 63, IIT Bombay

National Standard Examination in Physics (NSEP), conducted every year since 1987, is a voluntary examination given by more than 40,000 students at over 1200 centres, most of which are in small towns and villages. The participation, purely voluntary, offers an opportunity to measure one's standing against a national standard. NSEP is not linked to any entrance or scholarship award. Students are allowed to take away the question paper and the solutions are provided to them after a week. The NSEP is carried out in English, Hindi & a few other Indian languages.

Physics in NSEP exam is very close to physics in JEE exam(both advanced and mains). Attempting NSEP is always a good thing if one is preparing for JEE advanced.The problems will be slightly intriguing and stimulating to do in new methods.So after trying for sometime I suggest to look at the solution,learn the method.The same methods can be used over many places in physics. Attempt them on chapter basis.

Dedicate yourself in doing these problems for like once in a week in a specific time of your choice. Once you learned the concepts and solved decent number of problems from standard textbooks like H.C Verma then attempt to solve question from following books.The material recommended for NSEP are:
1)Previous year's question papers (from official IAPT website)
2)Indian national physics Olympiad by Saurabh.A from arihant publication.
3)Problems in general physics by I.E irodov.
