How to Study in the last week before JEE Mains

By Anonymous

One of the biggest challenges for JEE aspirants is revision of all the topics just before the exam. How to cover such a big syllabus once again!!.

So I'm going to tell you one of the many methods to revise and hopes it might help you.

1. Revise old questions (done by you in the past):
One thing JEE aspirant need to know is that at the time of revision one must do quality questions rather than doing a bulk of questions. Here quality questions mean that one must do one or two questions belonging to individual subtopics. Also do the star marked (marked by you as important) questions which you couldn't do at first attempt. Do not try to cover each and everything at the last moment. Just solve previous years' questions, etc


2. Never start any new topic in the Last week:
In the last week before the exam, never start any major topic that you haven’t studied yet. For instance, people start studying inorganic reactions in the last week. PLEASE DON’T DO THAT. Starting new topics may increase you anxiety and stress levels. Light revision is okay though.

3. Gradually reduce your hours of study:
In the last week you should try focus more on core concepts instead of trying new questions. Gradually reduce your time of daily studies and try spending time with family and playing outdoor games (just make sure you don’t hurt yourself). This will increase the positive vibrations within you. Relax, watch light Comedy Movies, and comedy TV series. This will help you to reduce your pressure off.

4. Having a proper sleep is very important in last week:
Even though having a proper sleep during the whole course of JEE is important but it is really very much crucial in the last week before the exam. Many studies show that sleeping helps in organising memory and improves brain’s capabilities.

5. Have Confidence
Lastly, have a lot of confidence in yourself and crack the exam. All the best.

Hope my article might help..........
